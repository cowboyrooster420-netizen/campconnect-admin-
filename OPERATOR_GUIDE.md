# Campfire — Operator Guide

*The year-round camp engagement platform. This is the plain-English guide to what
Campfire is, why it exists, and how you run it.*

---

## 1. Why Campfire exists

Every summer your camp creates something rare: 8–10 weeks of intense belonging,
friendship, and identity. Kids leave transformed. And then… **the connection
fades.** By the time enrollment opens again, the magic is a distant memory, and
you're starting from cold every year.

That fade costs you in three ways:

- **Lower re-enrollment** — campers (and their parents) move on.
- **Weaker referrals** — the emotional high that makes kids evangelize wears off.
- **Lost identity** — "I'm a camper" stops being part of who they are between summers.

**Campfire's bet:** if you keep that connection warm *all year* — in small,
structured, genuinely fun doses — campers stay emotionally invested, beg their
parents to come back, and bring their friends. Even a modest lift in returning
campers is significant revenue for a camp.

So Campfire turns your summer into a **year-round engagement loop**.

---

## 2. The core idea

Take the things that made summer special — the **counselors**, the **traditions**,
the **challenges**, the sense of **belonging** — and deliver them to campers'
phones in a structured rhythm across the off-season.

Three principles shape everything:

1. **Counselor-driven authenticity.** The emotional pull isn't an app — it's the
   *counselors*. Kids hear from the staff they love, in the places they remember.
2. **Structured, predictable loops.** Not an endless feed to doom-scroll. A clear
   cadence: a challenge drops, a counselor nudges, kids participate, they earn a
   patch. Repeat.
3. **It is NOT a social network.** No camper posts, no likes, no comments, no
   public feed of kids. This is deliberate. It keeps kids safe, keeps the camp in
   control of the experience, and keeps the focus on *camp*, not on each other's
   popularity. Campfire is a **broadcast channel from the camp to its campers** —
   one direction.

---

## 3. The cast of characters (glossary)

**Star Counselor**
A small set of your most charismatic, beloved counselors — the ones campers
idolize. During summer, with help from your media staff, they capture short,
lightweight videos: hyping up challenges, checking in, recapping. These videos
are the heartbeat of Campfire. A camper opening the app to see "their" counselor
saying their name is the entire emotional engine. *You only need a handful of
star counselors and a little summer footage to power the whole year.*

**Challenge**
A structured activity a camper does at home during the off-season — recreate a
camp meal, tie three knots, write a gratitude letter, do the polar plunge. They
come from a shared **library** of templates (outdoor / creative / reflection /
tradition). You pick which ones to run and when. Plan on roughly **one challenge
per week** during your active season.

**The three beats of a challenge**
Every challenge unfolds like a mini-campaign:
- 🎬 **Intro** — the star counselor introduces and hypes the challenge.
- 👋 **Nudge** — a few days later, a counselor pops up *in a familiar camp spot*
  ("Hey — done the Camp Song one yet? I'm headed to the dining hall for lunch…").
  It re-engages campers who drifted, and sneaks in a hit of camp nostalgia.
- 🏁 **Wrap-up** — at the end, a counselor recaps and celebrates.

**Submission**
What a camper turns in for a challenge — a photo, a short video, or written text.
One per camper per challenge.

**Patch (badge)**
The reward. Completing a challenge earns a collectible **patch** — a medallion in
the camper's collection. There are **no points and no leaderboards** — just a
growing shelf of patches that represents the camper's identity and history.
(More on why in §7.)

**The Feed**
The camper's home screen — the "camp channel." Intro videos, nudges, wrap-ups,
and announcements all stream here. One-way, no comments.

**Announcement**
Camp news that isn't tied to a challenge — a newsletter drop, registration
opening, an event. A cover image, a headline, and a button that can link out
(to your newsletter, sign-up page, social, etc.).

**The Countdown**
A "Camp starts in 87 days" ticker at the top of the camper's feed. A simple,
powerful hook — kids see it shrink and nag their parents to re-enroll.

## 4. How it works — the two loops

**The camper's loop (what kids experience):**
1. Open the app → see the **feed** (counselor videos, nudges, the countdown).
2. Tap into a **challenge** → watch the counselor, read what to do.
3. **Do it** at home → submit a photo/video/text.
4. You **review** it → it's approved.
5. They **earn the patch** → their collection grows.
6. A few days later, the next challenge drops. The rhythm pulls them back.

**Your loop (what you run — the "engagement operator"):**
1. **Capture** star-counselor content over the summer (lightweight, during normal
   camp operations).
2. **Schedule** challenges across the off-season and attach the videos.
3. **Review** submissions as they come in (approve → patch is awarded automatically).
4. **Post** announcements and keep the countdown current.

The whole point is that step-by-step it's **low effort** for you. The hard part
(authentic counselor content) is captured once, in summer, when everyone's
already there.

---

## 5. Capturing content over the summer

This is the one thing that takes a little planning — and it pays off all year.

- Pick your **star counselors** (charisma over seniority).
- For each challenge you plan to run, capture three short clips with each
  counselor: an **intro**, a **nudge** (filmed somewhere iconic on camp — the
  dining hall, the waterfront, the campfire ring), and a **wrap-up**.
- Keep them short, genuine, and a little goofy. Polish is not the point —
  *the counselor being themselves* is the point.
- The nudge scripts are where the **nostalgia** lives: have counselors reference
  shared summer memories, inside jokes, and camp places.

A camper who left in August and sees their favorite counselor in February,
standing by the lake, asking if they've done the challenge yet — *that's* the
moment that brings them back.

## 6. Running it in the dashboard

Everything below happens in the operator dashboard.

**Set up a challenge (the "campaign")**
- Go to **Challenges** → browse the library (each card explains what the challenge
  actually is) → **Add to season**.
- Tap the challenge to open its editor. There you:
  - Set the **release date** (the anchor everything schedules against).
  - Upload the **🎬 intro video**.
  - Add one or more **👋 nudges** — each with a counselor video and "drops *N*
    days after release." Set up the whole arc once; releasing the challenge
    schedules the nudges automatically.
  - Upload the **🏁 wrap-up video**.
- Hit **Go live** when you're ready. The intro hits the feed; nudges follow on
  schedule; campers can submit.

**Review submissions**
- The **Review queue** shows everything campers have turned in.
- Preview the photo/video/text → **Approve** or **Send back**.
- Approving automatically awards the patch — you don't manage points or badges by hand.

**Post announcements**
- In **Feed**, post an announcement: a cover photo (or pick an illustrated scene),
  a badge label ("JUST POSTED"), a headline, and an optional button that links out.
- Schedule it for now or a future date.

**Keep the countdown current**
- On the **Overview**, set your **next session start date**. The "Camp starts in
  N days" ticker does the rest. Once a summer ends, point it at the next one.

## 7. The reward model — patches, not points

Campfire deliberately uses a **collection of patches** instead of a points score.
Here's why it matters:

- **Identity, not competition.** A points leaderboard pits kids against each
  other and ages out fast. A patch shelf is *personal* — it's a record of *your*
  camp journey. That's the feeling we want to grow.
- **It compounds year over year.** Patches don't reset. A camper returning for
  their fourth summer opens the app to a wall of patches from four years — and
  *that* pride is exactly what drives re-enrollment. (Tenure/"X-year camper"
  patches are a natural next step.)
- **Every challenge earns one.** Finish a challenge → earn its patch. Plus a
  welcome patch just for joining.

Patch names are yours to set — "Firekeeper," "Trailblazer," "Polar Bear" — so the
collection can feel like *your* camp.

---

## 8. Cadence & best practices

- **One challenge a week** during your active off-season keeps a steady pulse
  without overwhelming kids (or you).
- **Front-load the work.** Capture all your counselor content in summer; schedule
  the season in a couple of sittings; then it mostly runs itself.
- **Lean into the nudge.** It's the highest-leverage, most emotional moment — a
  counselor in a camp place, talking *to* the camper. Don't skip it.
- **Review promptly.** A fast "approved!" + patch keeps the loop tight and the kid
  coming back.
- **Use announcements for the funnel.** Newsletter drops, "registration is OPEN,"
  early-bird deadlines — with a button straight to your sign-up page.

---

## 9. The bottom line

Campfire keeps your camp's magic alive in the 10 months kids *aren't* there — with
the counselors they love, the traditions they remember, and a growing collection
of patches that says *"I am a camper, all year long."*

Do that well, and re-enrollment and referrals take care of themselves.
